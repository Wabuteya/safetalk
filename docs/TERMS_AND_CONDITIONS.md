## Important notice

SafeTalk is a **supplementary** mental health support platform. It is **not** a crisis intervention service, ambulance service, or substitute for emergency medical care.

**If you are experiencing a mental health emergency** or are in immediate danger, contact **emergency services without delay**. In Uganda, national emergency lines commonly include **999** and **112**; use whatever number your locality designates for police, ambulance, or combined emergency response. You may also use **on-campus and local helplines shown inside SafeTalk** (for example, under *Emergency Contacts* or *Crisis Support*).

**SafeTalk** (the **“Platform”**) is the official mental health support platform of **Uganda Christian University** (**“UCU,” “University,” “we,” “us,” “our”**). It is designed to connect **eligible UCU students** with **professional university-affiliated therapists** in a secure, confidential, and—where the features so provide—**anonymous** digital environment.

By accessing or using SafeTalk, you confirm that you have **read**, **understood**, and **agree** to be bound by these Terms and Conditions (**“Terms”**). If you do not agree, you must not use the Platform.

These Terms apply to **all** users of SafeTalk, including:

- **Students** registered at UCU who are granted access;
- **Licensed therapists** employed or contracted by UCU and authorised to use the Platform; and  
- **System administrators** and other **UCU staff** authorised to manage or support the Platform.

Additional policies, employment contracts, or clinical governance documents may apply to therapists and staff.

## 2. Eligibility and registration

### 2.1 Student eligibility

SafeTalk is intended for **currently enrolled students of Uganda Christian University**. By registering, you confirm that:

- You are an **active UCU student** with a **valid student registration** (or other identifier required at registration);
- You are **at least 18 years of age**, or you have obtained **parental or guardian consent** as required by law and UCU policy;
- You will provide **accurate** registration information and **update** it when it changes, where the Platform allows;
- You will **keep your login credentials confidential** and not allow others to use your account.

### 2.2 Anonymous identity

The Platform may assign each student a **unique anonymous alias** (for example, a memorable nickname). That alias may appear in parts of the experience to protect your identity in day-to-day use. **Your real name, contact details, and student identifiers** are stored subject to **Section 4** and may be accessed only in **verified emergency circumstances** as described in **Section 5**.

### 2.3 Therapist eligibility

Therapist accounts are for **licensed mental health professionals** employed or **expressly approved** by UCU. Therapist accounts are **created and managed** by UCU administration (or delegated technical roles). Therapists must comply with professional standards, UCU policies, and these Terms insofar as they apply to their use of the Platform.

## 3. Platform services

SafeTalk may offer the following (availability of a given feature may depend on your role and UCU configuration):

### 3.1 Anonymous journaling

Students may maintain a **private digital journal**. Entries are **private by default**. You may **choose to share specific entries** with your **assigned therapist**. Where the product is designed such that **shared entries cannot be withdrawn (“unshared”)**, that action may be **permanent** and technically or policy-wise **irreversible**—you should share only what you intend your therapist to retain. Therapists may read **only** journal content you have **explicitly shared**, subject to lawful access in emergencies (**Section 5**).

### 3.2 Mood tracking

You may log emotional wellbeing using the **scale and labels provided in the app** (for example, from lowest to highest mood categories). Mood data is intended to give **context** to your assigned therapist. **Mood data does not, by itself, automatically trigger clinical alerts or escalations** unless UCU configures specific workflows that are clearly disclosed to you; absent such disclosure, treat mood logging as **supportive context**, not a clinical monitoring system.

### 3.3 Secure messaging

The Platform may provide **in-platform messaging** between you and your assigned therapist. **Messaging is not guaranteed to be monitored in real time.** Unless UCU states otherwise in a dedicated privacy or security notice, assume messages are **protected in transit and at rest using UCU-approved technical measures**, but are **not necessarily end-to-end encrypted** in a way that excludes system administrators from lawful access. **Do not use messaging for life-threatening emergencies**; use emergency numbers and local services.

### 3.4 Appointment booking

You may book sessions with your assigned therapist according to **published availability**. Confirmations may be sent to your **registered email**. You should **cancel or reschedule** as far in advance as reasonably possible—**at least 24 hours** where feasible. **Repeated no-shows** without notice may lead to **review of your access** to booking or to the Platform, at UCU’s reasonable discretion.

### 3.5 Support resources

The Platform may provide **articles, tools, videos, and links** curated or uploaded by UCU therapists. **External links** are provided for convenience; UCU is **not responsible** for third-party content, privacy practices, or availability. Media may play inside the Platform where technically supported.

### 3.6 Crisis support

A **crisis support** feature may allow you to **signal urgent need for help**. Alerts may be delivered to your **assigned therapist** and **authorised UCU administration**. Crisis workflows are **supplementary** to emergency services—not a replacement. In **verified emergencies** as described in **Section 5**, **limited identity disclosure** may occur so that appropriate help can be coordinated (**Section 5.1**).

## 4. Privacy, confidentiality, and data

These Terms summarise key expectations; the **Privacy Policy** (when published) and UCU’s data-protection practices **also apply** and should be read together with this document.

### 4.1 Categories of data

Depending on how you use SafeTalk, UCU may process categories such as:

- **Registration data**: name, student ID, email, phone, and related identifiers;
- **Anonymous alias** assigned by or for the Platform;
- **Journal entries** (private and shared);
- **Mood logs**;
- **Messages** between you and your therapist;
- **Appointments** and related notifications;
- **Usage metadata** (for example, login times, features used) for security and improvement;
- **Derived insights** (for example, **sentiment or trend summaries** from **shared** journal content) where such features exist.

### 4.2 Purposes of processing

Data is used to **operate and improve** SafeTalk, **connect students with therapists**, generate **therapist-visible contextual or trend information** where the product provides it, send **operational communications** (such as appointments), respond to **crisis circumstances** as set out in **Section 5**, and maintain **security** and **integrity** of the Platform.

### 4.3 Sharing

UCU does **not sell** your personal data. Access is limited to:

- Your **assigned therapist**, within the scope of what the Platform exposes to them (for example, **alias**, **shared** journals, mood, messages, appointments);
- **Authorised UCU administration** for legitimate management, security, safeguarding, and compliance; and  
- Others **only where required by Ugandan law**, **regulation**, or a **lawful court or administrative order**, or where **explicitly permitted** under an approved policy you have been told about.

### 4.4 Retention and deletion

Unless UCU adopts a different published schedule, data may be retained for **the duration of your enrolment** at UCU **plus a reasonable period** (for example, **up to two (2) years** after graduation or withdrawal) for lawful, safeguarding, or administrative purposes, after which it is **securely deleted** in line with UCU policy.

You may **request deletion** of your account or certain data by contacting **UCU administration** as in **Section 12**. Some records may need to be **retained longer** where the law, professional standards, or serious incident investigation require it.

### 4.5 AI / sentiment analysis

Where the Platform uses **automated analysis** (for example, **sentiment** or **emotional trend** features) on **shared** journal text:

- Outputs are intended **only** for **contextual awareness** for therapists;
- They are **not** a **diagnosis** and **must not** replace **professional clinical judgment**;
- They should be presented and understood as **supplementary** and, where applicable, **labelled** as **AI-generated** or similar.

## 5. Emergency and crisis protocol

### 5.1 When real identity may be accessed

Strong **anonymity** protections apply in normal use. However, where there is a **credible risk to life or serious harm** to you or others, **authorised UCU therapists or administrators** may access **identifying and contact information** **solely** to **coordinate help**. This is the **exception** to routine anonymity—not the rule.

### 5.2 Examples of emergency situations

Examples may include (non-exhaustive): **suicidal ideation with intent or plan**; **self-harm** requiring **medical attention**; **credible threats** to harm others; or other circumstances where **immediate physical intervention** or emergency services are reasonably required. **Final judgment** rests with qualified professionals and UCU policy.

### 5.3 Crisis alerts

Activating crisis support may generate an **alert** logged for **assigned therapist(s)** and **authorised administration**. **Response times are not guaranteed**. Where UCU sets internal expectations (for example, **acknowledgement during working hours**), those are **aspirational** and depend on staffing and capacity. **Crisis alerts do not replace** calling **999 / 112** or other **emergency services** when seconds count.

## 6. User responsibilities and acceptable use

### 6.1 Student responsibilities

You agree to:

- Use the Platform **honestly** and in **good faith**;
- **Safeguard** credentials and **not share** your account;
- Use **messaging** and **crisis** features **responsibly** and **only when genuinely needed**;

- **Manage appointments** responsibly (**Section 3.4**);
- **Report** suspected security issues or misuse to **UCU administration**.

### 6.2 Prohibited conduct

You must **not**, for example:

- Attempt to **de-anonymise** other students or **access** data that is not yours;
- Misuse **crisis** features, including **false** or **nuisance** alerts;
- Upload **illegal**, **abusive**, **harassing**, or **harmful** content;
- **Circumvent** security, **scrape** the Platform without permission, or use it for **unauthorised commercial** purposes;
- **Harass**, **threaten**, or **abuse** therapists or staff.

### 6.3 Therapist responsibilities

Therapists agree to maintain **professional** conduct, **protect confidentiality**, respond to **crisis workflows** in line with UCU policy, use **notes and observations ethically**, **not disclose** student information outside **authorised UCU channels**, and keep **profile and availability** information current.
